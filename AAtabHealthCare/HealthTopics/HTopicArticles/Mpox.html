Mpox (Monkeypox) 🦠

Mpox (formerly known as monkeypox) is a rare but potentially serious viral infection. It spreads through close contact with an infected person or contaminated materials. Symptoms include fever, rash, and swollen lymph nodes.

What is Mpox? ❓

Mpox is caused by the monkeypox virus, which belongs to the same family as the variola virus (which causes smallpox). Although less severe than smallpox, it can still lead to complications in some cases.

How Does It Spread? 🔄

Mpox spreads through direct skin-to-skin contact, respiratory droplets, or contact with contaminated objects such as bedding or clothing.

Symptoms of Mpox ⚠️

  • 🤒 Fever, chills
  • 😵‍💫 Headache, muscle aches
  • 🦠 Swollen lymph nodes
  • 🟥 Rash that progresses from red spots to blisters
  • 😴 Fatigue

How to Prevent Mpox 🛡️

  • 🚫 Avoid close contact with infected individuals
  • 🧼 Wash hands frequently with soap and water
  • 🩺 Use personal protective equipment when necessary
  • 💉 Get vaccinated if at high risk

Treatment for Mpox 💊

There is no specific treatment for Mpox, but supportive care can help. In severe cases, antiviral medications may be used.

When to Seek Medical Help 🏥

  • 🩹 If you develop a rash with flu-like symptoms
  • 🤝 If you have been in contact with a confirmed case
  • 📈 If your symptoms worsen over time
