Tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious bacterial infection caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It primarily affects the lungs but can also spread to other parts of the body, including the kidneys, spine, and brain. If left untreated, TB can be life-threatening.

TB is an airborne disease that spreads when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or speaks, releasing bacteria into the air. While some people may carry TB bacteria without symptoms (latent TB), others develop active TB, which requires immediate treatment.
